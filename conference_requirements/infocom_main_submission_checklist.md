# INFOCOM Main-Conference Submission Checklist

Working rule basis: INFOCOM 2026 public main-conference instructions, checked on 2026-06-15.

## Length and Format

- Maximum submission length: 10 printed pages total.
- Main text limit: no more than 9 pages, including figures, tables, appendices, and all material other than references.
- References may occupy the remaining space up to page 10.
- Language: English.
- Format: standard IEEE Transactions/IEEEtran conference format.
- LaTeX class: unmodified `IEEEtran.cls` version 1.8.
- Required LaTeX preamble: `\documentclass[10pt, conference, letterpaper]{IEEEtran}`.
- Do not override margins, font size, line spacing, column spacing, font type, or other default IEEEtran formatting choices.
- PDF must be text-searchable.
- Figures must be clear and legible in black-and-white printing.
- The manuscript must be self-contained; reviewers are not required to read external technical reports, Dropbox/OneDrive links, or supplementary material.

## Double-Blind Requirements

- Remove author names and affiliations from the title page.
- Remove acknowledgments of identifying names and funding sources.
- Remove project titles or public project names that can identify the authors through search.
- Avoid identifying file names embedded in the generated PDF.
- Remove author, affiliation, license, and other identifying metadata from the PDF.
- Do not omit relevant prior work to preserve anonymity.
- If citing the authors' prior work, cite it in the third person like any other work.
- Do not use "reference deleted for double-blind review."

## Ethics and Human-Subject Work

- Work involving experiments on or with humans or animals, or analysis/presentation of personally identifiable information including aggregated data, must have appropriate institutional approval.
- The paper must include an explicit approval statement, anonymized for review when needed.
- The paper should consider broader research ethics and possible negative outcomes.

## Submission Process and Metadata

- Submit through EDAS as PDF.
- EDAS metadata must include title, all coauthors, and an abstract of up to 200 words.
- The PDF must not include author information in the title page, body, or metadata during review.
- The complete author list in EDAS must be correct before the submission deadline.
- The author list may not be changed after submission, except before the submission deadline.

## Preprints and Extended Reports

- Authors may post a preprint while the paper is under INFOCOM submission, but should avoid broad advertising.
- The submitted paper should not refer to an extended or technical report, even if anonymous.
- All essential technical content must fit within the page limit.
