# Originality, Plagiarism, and Workshop-Extension Checklist

Working rule basis: INFOCOM 2026 public submission instructions and IEEE policy links referenced by that page.

## Hard Originality Constraint

- INFOCOM states that only original papers that have not been published or submitted for publication elsewhere will be considered.
- Before submission, confirm with all coauthors whether the accepted CHASE/mHealthSec workshop paper is formally published, in press, or only accepted.
- If the workshop paper is or will be published, treat the INFOCOM manuscript as a new paper that must contain substantial new technical contribution, not merely expanded prose.
- Do not submit overlapping manuscripts simultaneously to multiple venues.

## Self-Plagiarism and Text Reuse

- Avoid copying large blocks from the workshop version into the longer paper.
- Reuse necessary technical definitions only where unavoidable, and rewrite surrounding explanation substantially.
- Add new problem framing, methodology detail, experiments, analysis, threat/ethics discussion, and limitations rather than padding existing sections.
- Track which sections materially differ from the workshop version.

## Prior-Version Citation Strategy

- If the workshop version becomes public before INFOCOM submission, cite it as prior work when required by policy and scientific transparency.
- For double-blind review, refer to prior work in third person and do not reveal that it is by the same authors.
- Do not use anonymous placeholders such as "reference omitted for review."
- If citing the prior workshop paper would break anonymity or violate venue-specific extension rules, get advisor/coauthor approval before submission.

## Plagiarism Avoidance

- Every claim about external systems, datasets, methods, or benchmark results must be cited to a precise source.
- Do not paraphrase source abstracts or introductions closely.
- Do not copy review text, CFP text, or external paper text into the manuscript.
- Check that all reference entries match the cited work: author list, title, venue, year, DOI or URL where applicable.

## Expansion Target

To make the nine-page main text defensible, the expanded manuscript should add real content in these areas:

- A clearer task definition for continuous, sentence-level, open-vocabulary fingerspelling recognition.
- A more detailed virtual-accelerometer construction method, including coordinate handling, filtering, temporal resampling, and failure modes.
- A more complete model description, including encoder/decoder choices, training objective, decoding, and adaptation.
- A stronger experimental protocol, including dataset splits, baselines, hyperparameters, metrics, and reproducibility details.
- Additional analysis beyond the workshop version: virtual-to-real gap, sensor-placement implications, failure cases, privacy/security/ethics, and deployment constraints.
